Galaxies

Galaxies are vast systems of dust, gas and dark matter. They contain from millions to trillions of stars and are held together by gravity. Around two thirds of known galaxies have a spiral shape with nearly all galaxies thought to have black holes at their center. The shapes of galaxies are influened by their neighbouring galaxies.



Stars

Stars are massive celestial bodies made up of Hydrogen and Helium. Stars produce light and heat due to the thermo-nuclear fusion of hydrogen and helium at their core. The stars we see in the night sky are light-years from Earth. The nearest star to Earth is the Sun.



Nebulae

A nebula (plural nebulae) is a huge cloud of dust and gas in space. The word nebula is derived from the latin word for cloud. Some nebulae are formed from the gas and dust that is released by the explosion of a dying star. Other nebulae are regions where new stars are beginning to form.
